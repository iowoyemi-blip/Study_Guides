How to write math notation (LaTeX)
Wrap math expressions in dollar signs: $ ... $. The preview below each question shows what students will see.
$x^2$
renders as $x^2$
$\sqrt{48}$
renders as $\sqrt{48}$
$\frac{1}{2}$
renders as $\frac{1}{2}$
$\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$
renders as $\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$
$y = ax^2 + bx + c$
renders as $y = ax^2 + bx + c$
$x_1, y_2$
renders as $x_1, y_2$ (subscripts)
$a \cdot b$
renders as $a \cdot b$ (dot product)
$\geq, \leq, \neq, \pm$
renders as $\geq, \leq, \neq, \pm$
Plain text without dollar signs is left alone, so existing questions don't need to change.
