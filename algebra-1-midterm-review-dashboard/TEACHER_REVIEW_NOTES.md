# Algebra I Midterm Review Dashboard - Teacher Review Notes

The dashboard was built from the Midterm Exam folder:

`Teaching-Studio/90-Archive/2025-2026/Instructional-Materials/Algebra-I_On_Level-Materials/Midterm Exam/`

## Source Files

- Student packet: `Algebra I Midterm Study Guide.pdf`
- Word packet: `Algebra I Midterm Study Guide.docx`

The source PDF and DOCX files were not changed.

## Included Problems

The app includes all 42 study guide problems:

- Unit 1: problems 1-4
- Unit 2: problems 5-13
- Unit 3: problems 14-23
- Unit 4: problems 24-42

The packet page preview uses rendered images from the 14-page student study guide, including the answer-key pages.

## Similar Practice

The similar-practice section now generates outside-the-packet practice instead of routing students to nearby problems in the paper packet.

Each major midterm review skill has at least four similar prompts available through the `New Similar Problem` button:

- exponent rules and radical simplification
- order of operations, equations, literal equations, and inequalities
- translating expressions and solving rectangle/perimeter word problems
- functions, domain/range, and function notation
- slope, linear equations, special lines, intercepts, and linear contexts

## Answer-Key Notes

The midterm study guide includes an embedded final-answer key on pages 12-14. Those final answers were used as the source of truth for the dashboard.

Unlike the unit study guide keys, this midterm packet does not include full worked solution steps. The "Solution Guidance" panels in the app are concise checking prompts written to help students understand how to verify their work. They should be reviewed if you want them to function as a fully official worked key.

The typed checker for similar-practice items is intentionally lightweight. It accepts common compact formats, but equivalent expression, graph, coordinate, interval, radical, or inequality notation may still need students to reveal the answer and compare manually.

## Student Workflow

1. Students complete problems on the paper packet first.
2. Students click the problem number in the dashboard.
3. Students reveal the final answer first.
4. Students reveal guidance only after checking their own work.
5. Students can use generated similar practice for extra outside-the-packet repetition.
